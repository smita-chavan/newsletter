Aims and scope

Journal of Ethnic Foods provides in-depth reporting on the consumption of food all over the world, with the aim of highlighting the benefits of traditional understanding and knowledge of foods.

Published by BMC and supported by the Korea Food Research Institute, this journal is a synthesis of insights on food gastronomy, eating behaviors, and food traditions and cultures, and how they relate to specific geographical contexts, ethnicities, and societal and environmental factors. The journal has a broad scope, examining food and eating from the perspectives of ethnography, cultural anthropology, population health and well-being, biology, history, ecology, and geography.

An Open Access journal, Journal of Ethnic Foods welcomes articles in these diverse areas of study, especially those highlighting the multidisciplinary nature of the study of ethnic food.
Message from the Editor-in-Chief

Our journal fully recognizes and lays heavy emphasis on the importance of a diverse, inclusive, and multidisciplinary approach to studying food, eating behaviors, and heritage.
Hyung-Hee Baek, Ph.D. Editor-in-Chief
President of the Korea
Food Research Institute
This journal looks beyond a research informed solely by biology, technology, and industrial engineering, and seeks a more comprehensive paradigm to answer a broader question—what role is food going to play in our societies in the future, especially in the post-industrialized era? We believe that looking at food from a wider perspective in terms of behaviors and well being, ethnicity, traditions, the environment, and cultural heritage is as important as other approaches in food research today. We hope that more food scientists and countries will take a greater interest in the importance of food analyzed from this interdisciplinary perspective in the Journal of Ethnic Foods.

Our representative research

Korean diet: Characteristics and historical background

Due to the growing international interest in the Korean diet (K-diet), researchers undertook to establish cohesive definitions and concepts in K-diet-related dialogue by compiling a list of the 100 most representative Korean dishes based on their role in tradition, culture, and health promotion.
Namul, the driving force behind health and high vegetable consumption in Korea

In this review, researchers provide an overview on Namuls—foods made by seasoning edible plants or leaves—an essential component of traditional Korean meals and the primary reason for the health, longevity, and high vegetable consumption of Koreans.
The impact of the Green Revolution on the indigenous crops of India

In this review, researchers throw light on how the Green Revolution affected the production of indigenous crops, as well as society, environment, nutrition and the per capita availability of foods. They also suggest ways to reinvigorate the production of indigenous crops.
Indonesian food culture mapping: A starter contribution to promote Indonesian culinary tourism

In this review, researchers have delved deep into the history, development, and longevity of Indonesian food culture and its transition into a deeply embedded multi-generational, societal value.
